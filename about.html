During my years in college, I collected a knowledge of interests within the creative arts. I enjoyed cinema and analyzing its content but discovered a love for theatre as I developed my study within its concentration. Theatre allowed me to become exposed to all works of different playwrights and composers from plays to musicals. I volunteered every evening and weekend to participating in backstage theatre. It allowed me to undertand how different departments involved themselves with each other in order to orchestrate an entire production in time for opening doors. I spent most of my personal time in stagecraft and lights. In stagecraft, we'd focus on constructing sets to scale and within the light deparment, I'd be assigned the spotlight to learn in order to operate it following noted cues for most of the musicals we staged. I floated around each department and assisted stage managers in a couple productions but I found my favorite spaces to collaborate in were within the stagecraft and organizing - as in stage managing and directing - departments.

While studying abroad my first semester sophomore year in 2016, I took a creative writing class for the first time and found a love for creative writing. I've always excelled in research writing thanks to one high school English professor but never correlated it to fiction. It was in my creative writing class and workshops where the professor and classmates would express their love for my stye of writing. I was never aware that I had skill or style in writing so I was surprised every time it was pointed out. I didn't really consider it until we workshoped stories. I would take my laptop to the library and enjoy applying my imagination and creating a world from scratch. I honestly found enjoyment in how descriptive my writing could play out alongside a theasurus on hand.
